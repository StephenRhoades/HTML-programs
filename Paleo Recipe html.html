Everyday Paleo Pizza

  • Prep Time: 45 minnutes
  • Cook Time: 45-50 minutes
  • Serves: 5
Quantity Crust Ingredients
2 cups almond meal
2 eggs
3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
1/4 teaspoon backing soda
1 teaspoon garlic powder
1 1/2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, minced
Quantity Toppings
1 pound mild italian pork sausage, casings removed
2 small zucchini or yellow summer squash, diced
3 green onions, diced
1 cup diced fresh basil leaves
2 Roma tomatoes, diced
1/2 cup jarred roasted red pepper, drained and diced
1/2 cup sliced black olives
1 cup organic, gluten-free marinara sauce
~optional mushrooms

  1. Preheat oven to 350Â°F.

  2. Using a spoon, mix all curst ingredients together until it becomes very thick. Using your hands, form the dough into a ball. If the dough is too sticky, add more almond flour. I like to use the courser almond meal like what you will find from Trader Joe's or from Ammin Nut Company(www.amminnut.com)

  3. Lightly grease a pizza pan or a backing sheet with extra-virgin olive oil. Place the ball of dough in the center of your baking sheet or pizza pan and using your hands, push and pat the dough down intp the shape of a circle, making the dough as thin as possible. Your pizza will be about 12 inches across.

  4. Bake just the curst in your preheated oven for 20-minutes.

  5. While the crust is cooking, prepare your toppings. Brown the sausage in a large skillet and cut up alll the veggies.

  6. After the crust is done, remove from the oven and evenly spread the marinara sauce over the crust.

  7. Add the sausage and all the remaining toppings evenly over the sauce and bake again for an additional 25-30 minutes.

  8. Get creative and use whatever toppings you might like- I also suggest trying chicken, artichoke hearts, and broccoli!

Dry Rub Burgers

  • Prep Time: 25 minnutes
  • Cook Time: 8-10 minutes
  • Serves: 6-7
Quantity Ingredients
1 1/2 teaspoons chili powder
2 1/2 tespoons paprika
1 teaspoon ground cumin
1/4 teaspoon cayenne powder
1/4 teaspoon black pepper
2 pounds ground beef (makes about 7-9 medium- sized burgers)
to taste sea salt

  1. Mix all dry rub ingredients together in a medium-size bowl.

  2. Form hamburger meat into patties and place the patties one burger at a time into the dry rub, covering both sides generously with the seasoning mixture.

  3. Let the seasoned burgers sit in the fridge for about 30 minutes before cooking.

  4. Either grill or pan fry for about 4 minutes each side for medium, less for rare, more for well.

Chicken with Mushroom Basil Cream Sauce

  • Prep Time: 20 minnutes
  • Cook Time: 15 minutes
  • Serves: 3-4
Quantity Ingredients
3 boneless skinless chicken breasts
to taste sea salt and black pepper
2 tablespoons coconut oil
2 tablespoons chicken broth
2 cups sliced mushrooms
1/4 cup full-fat canned coconut milk
2 teaspoons dried basil
2 green onions, diced for garnish

  1. Using a meat mallet, pound out the chicken breasts so each is about 1/2 inch thick.

  2. Heat the coconut oil in a saucepan over medium to medium-high heat.

  3. Season the chicken with the salt and pepper. Place the chicken into the pan once the oil is nice and hot.

  4. Brown the chicken on both sides, and then remove chicken from pan and set aside.

  5. To the same pan add chicken broth, mushrooms, coconut milk, and basil.

  6. Whisk together and simmer until the sauce thickens.

  7. Season the sauce with salkt and pepper to taste.

  8. Pour the sauce from the pan over the chicken and sprinkle chopped green onions on top.
